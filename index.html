1. Prepare the poolish

Mix the cold or room-temperature poolish water, flour, instant dry yeast, and honey in a container. Let it rest at room temperature for 1 hour, then move it to the fridge for 16 to 24 hours.

2. Mix the main dough

Take the poolish out of the fridge. Add the main dough water, flour, and salt. Mix thoroughly until it forms a smooth, cohesive dough.

3. Bulk rest

Cover the dough and let it rest at room temperature for about 1 hour to relax the gluten.

4. Ball up

Divide the dough and shape into tight 250g balls. Place them in a proofing box or cover them tightly to prevent drying out.

5. Final proof

Let the dough balls rest at room temperature for 2 to 3 hours. You will know they are ready when they are visibly puffy, extremely soft to the touch, and don't snap back when you gently poke them.

1. Mix water and yeast

Pour your cold or room-temperature water into a bowl and dissolve the instant dry yeast completely.

2. Add flour and salt

In a separate bowl, mix your flour and salt together. Add to the water and yeast mix and combine.

3. Knead

Gradually add the rest of the flour. Knead for 15-20 minutes until the dough is smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky.

4. Bulk rest

Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and leave it at room temperature for 2 hours.

5. Ball up & final proof

Divide the dough into 250g pieces and form tight balls. Place them in a proofing box at room temperature for 6 to 8 hours before stretching and baking.
